## 5. Narrative Flow Assessment

### Orientation section — EXCELLENT

The reading order is pedagogically deliberate:

1. Entry / context (where-to-begin)
2. Philosophical stance before any evidence (manifesto)
3. Historical authority — names, five millennia (women-of-alchemy)
4. The cost of erasure — emotional and institutional context (the-grief)
5. The mechanism — how suppression was maintained (the-language-of-erasure)
6. Myth as coherent explanatory model (inanna)
7. Internal + external pharmacy (two-distillates, the-distillery-in-the-field)
8. Prerequisites — biological preparation (the-biological-baseline)
9. The empirical record — data with context (thesis)
10. Temporal integration — cyclical understanding (a-year-in-the-practice)

Logic: suppression → history → myth as explanation → mechanism → measurement → temporal context. Context before claims, history before theory, theory before measurement. This is excellent pedagogical architecture.

### Method section — GOOD

Setup prerequisites → diet foundation → raw practice → refined fractions → specific applications → vocabulary reference → recording tools. Clear progression. Dense but readable sequentially. Cross-references within the section are well-placed.

### Experiments → Analysis → Synthesis → Living Record — COHERENT, SLIGHTLY ASYMMETRICAL

The sequence works: two first-run experiments (Olivier + Ana) → structured water analysis → multiday synthesis essay → personal narrative → running log. The main asymmetry: only two formal experiment reports, both from February 2026. Later runs referenced in the thesis and practice log are not yet formally documented.

**The transition from sediment-field-guide to experiments is implicit** — readers are expected to understand that experiments follow naturally from method. A single sentence of bridge language in sediment-field-guide.md would clarify this jump.

## 10. Logical and Conceptual Gaps

These are natural limits of a living archive, not structural failures:

**Time scale gap:** The archive documents Feb–April 2026 in detail, but the 4-year practice period (2022–2025) is referenced only briefly in the living loop and thesis. Future practitioners may want pre-2026 records if they exist.

**Replication gap:** Only two formal experiment reports. The thesis references "Beloeil batch" and "60 litres stored" — no formal documentation of those runs. Future publication would be strengthened by 2–3 additional experiment reports showing pattern over time.

**Application gap:** Method section covers preparation and production in detail; documentation of sustained use outcomes is limited to brief mentions in the practice log. The oral-care protocol is the one specific application fully documented.

**Method → Experiments transition:** Implicit rather than explicit. A brief sentence at the end of sediment-field-guide.md pointing toward the experiments section would smooth this join.
