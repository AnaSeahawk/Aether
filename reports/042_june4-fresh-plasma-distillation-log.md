## Immediate Read

The pH held tightly across the jars at 9.3-9.4. That is the clearest stable feature in this batch.

Conductivity ranged from 705 to 1084 uS/cm, with TDS estimated from 347 to 542 ppm. By internal archive comparison, this sits closer to the March 6 primary fresh plasma distillation range than to the low-conductivity redistilled fraction. That comparison is only provisional because this June 4 batch lacks raw baseline readings and exact fraction tracking.

ORP was mildly negative where captured: -18 mV and -22 mV. These are near-neutral reducing readings, not a strong reducing signature by themselves.

H2 varied substantially: 0 to about 52 ppb. The variation does not track the pH, which stayed nearly unchanged. It may be tracking measurement timing, temperature, jar headspace, sealing, source mixture, meter stabilization, agitation, or some combination of those factors.

The freshest and warmest sample showed the highest captured H2 range. The oldest and coolest sample showed 0 ppb. That pattern is worth testing, but this run cannot separate age, temperature, jar handling, and source composition.

## What This Adds To The Archive

This is the first logged June 2026 fresh plasma distillation batch with H2 readings attached. The data strengthens one practical lesson: dissolved hydrogen needs tighter timing discipline than pH or conductivity. In this batch, alkalinity stayed stable while H2 moved sharply between jars.

The current record also supports keeping raw-source measurement as a required future step. Without the raw baseline, the archive can compare the distillates to one another, but it cannot say how much the distillation shifted the source material.

## Recommended Next Protocol

For the next fresh plasma run, label each jar before distillation and record:

1. Source identity category: Ana, partner, or blend.
2. Collection window: start time, end time, and approximate age at distillation.
3. Raw source readings: temp, pH, EC, TDS, ORP, and H2 if available.
4. Distillation timing: run start, first drip, jar change, and run end.
5. Jar condition: fill level, headspace, lid time, and whether the jar was opened before measurement.
6. Measurement timing: immediate, 15 min, 60 min, 3 hr, and 24 hr if possible.
7. Duplicate readings: at least two stabilized meter readings per variable.

For H2 retention specifically, temperature and time after distillation should be treated as primary variables. H2 may need a fixed measurement rhythm to become interpretable.

## Working Hypothesis

The stable pH suggests the alkaline volatile fraction was consistent across the four jars. The unstable H2 suggests dissolved gas retention is more fragile than the basic alkaline chemistry and may depend strongly on handling conditions.

The next clean run should test whether H2 decay follows time after distillation, cooling temperature, source composition, or jar handling.
