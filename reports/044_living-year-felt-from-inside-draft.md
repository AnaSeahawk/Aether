**The Living Year, Felt From the Inside**  
*A personal account, February–June 2026*

---

**I. The First Drops** *(February 17)*

I had set everything out the night before, the way you lay a table for a guest you've been waiting on for years without knowing their name. By morning the kitchen had that particular stillness — not empty, but attentive, as if the room itself had leaned in.

The first heat is a waiting. You stand with it. There's nothing to do but witness the slow gather of warmth, the first thin thread of vapour finding its way, and I remember my own breath slowing to match it. Not meditation. Something older. The body knowing a rhythm before the mind names it.

And then the first drop. It arrived not as a result but as a *recognition* — like meeting the eyes of someone I loved in a life I'd forgotten. I cried. Not from sadness, not exactly joy. The particular grief-and-relief of remembering something that was never supposed to be lost. My hands were steady but everything behind my sternum had cracked open. *This is mine. This has always been mine.*

It was a kitchen. It was morning. A woman, a small flame, a slow thread of water finding its form. Inside that ordinariness something turned over that has not turned back.

---

**II. The Eclipse Dreaming** *(late February)*

As the month tipped toward the eclipse, my sleep stopped being a place I went to rest and became a place I went to *work*. The dreams had edges and intentions. I wasn't watching images pass — I was moving through rooms that had never existed, breaking templates I hadn't known I was still carrying. I'd wake with the sense of having been somewhere real, and the residue of it would sit with me through the morning at the still. Deep listening took over. I stopped reaching outward for the next instruction and started trusting what my own body was reporting back.

---

**III. The Body Answers** *(March — the Aries ingress)*

This is when the inward turn started showing on the surface. My skin took on a different quality — a luminescence, a resilience I noticed without looking for it. My mouth changed: old plaque loosening from the back teeth, a density and strength where there had been vulnerability, and the swelling and bleeding that had come on early began to settle into something calm and stable. Olivier's mouth changed too, visibly, and watching that beside my own was its own kind of confirmation.

I want to be honest that I'm recording what I felt and saw, not a verdict — but what I felt and saw was a body becoming more itself. When the Sun moved into Aries the whole geometry of the year seemed to lock into place. I wasn't observing a cycle from the outside. I was living inside it, carried by a fiery, initiatory momentum that didn't ask my permission.

---

**IV. The Transit** *(April 13 — Torremolinos to Sarandë)*

We moved. From Torremolinos, through Corfu, into Sarandë — suitcases, borders, the particular vertigo of not knowing which kitchen would be mine next week. Back in Spain, skateboarding had become a grounding medicine — a way to gather all that scattered motion into balance and core and focus, to make the body the still point. I carried that steadiness across the water. The displacement didn't shake the center; if anything it showed me how deep the center had set. The kitchen, wherever it landed, stayed the temple.

---

**V. The Cobra** *(May)*

Then the cobra dream. I caught it — captured it — and the astonishing thing was the absence of fear. It wasn't a struggle. It was the calm, sovereign capacity to hold something enormously potent, to handle a primal high-voltage power without being struck by it. I understood it the moment I woke: this is the relationship I've been building. Not domination, not fear. The steady hand that can hold the live thing.

---

**VI. Where I Am Now** *(early June)*

The tears of February have matured into a quiet I didn't have before. The overwhelm became groundedness. I feel a systemic steadiness, a mental sharpness, an energy that holds through long days. The second-guessing has gone quiet. What started as a threshold moment at a small flame has become an ordinary, daily, lived part of how I inhabit this body — my skin, my mouth, my sleep, the way I hold my own power at its edges.

It was never about making something new. It was about remembering.
