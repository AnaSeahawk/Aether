## What Is Water Magicians

Water Magicians is the working name for a private three-person collaboration between **Ana Seahawk**, **Megan MacDonald**, and **Fiona Gardner**. It is a consent-led, community-supported living research archive for the revival of women's alchemical language and practice. The name is provisional — it holds the pilot rather than naming the finished structure.

The collaboration's archive lives in:

`Components/the-vessel/35-collaboration-private/water-magicians/`

Everything in this lane is private working material. Nothing is cleared for public use by default.

## The Three Collaborators And Their Roles

Each person brings a distinct doorway into the shared field:

**Ana Seahawk** — the archive, Mother Spirit, YouTube, Telegram, and the long living record of vessel practice. She holds the pressure to make the work coherent without flattening it.

**Megan MacDonald** — formal academic credentials, systems language, a practitioner field, a growing YouTube community, and a corrective instinct that protects the work from being over-westernized or falsely attributed. Her feedback on the AI-expanded systems document was a book-level boundary correction, not only an editorial note.

**Fiona Gardner** — microscopy, visual observation, Instagram, image language, and the capacity to describe movement, boundary, pattern, and living field from close direct observation. Her material gives the archive an observational vocabulary unlike anything available through text alone.

The collaboration becomes useful when these three functions stay distinct and in conversation. None of the three private lanes is the center. No one's community owns the shared field.

## The Proposed Structure: Water Magicians As A Shared Support Lane

Drafted June 29: [collective-archive-support-path-2026-06-29.md](../Components/the-vessel/35-collaboration-private/water-magicians/collective-archive-support-path-2026-06-29.md)

The proposal is a simple donation support path under the Water Magicians name. Its first purpose is to fund the ongoing research and archive labor. Each woman keeps her own house; the shared lane runs beside those containers.

**The basic principle:** Water Magicians supports what is genuinely shared — not any one woman's whole body of work.

**What the support lane pays for:** research, archive care, transcription, microscopy and image documentation, alchemical language recovery, meeting time, consent review, drafting, design, gathering preparation, and the relational field that keeps the archive from becoming isolated inside one person's head.

**The money boundary:** donation income split three ways by default; shared expenses paid first only if all three agree; no one's existing platform income is touched; no one is expected to contribute unpaid labor indefinitely without naming the money conversation.

**The first shape:** a support page with a clear statement of purpose, a simple way to contribute, and an explicit note that support does not buy access to private stories or private community spaces.

## The Living Journal Participation Template

Drafted July 1: [living-journal-participation-template-2026-07-01.md](../Components/the-vessel/35-collaboration-private/water-magicians/living-journal-participation-template-2026-07-01.md)

The template gives people a way to enter the archive without pretending their practice history begins today. It covers:

- contributor information and contact path
- consent level (four tiers, from private-archive-only to public-quotation-after-review)
- a "Bring Us Up To Speed" section (first contact, practice history, source and learning lineage, body and life context)
- an ongoing journal entry form (what happened, practice context, body record, alchemical language, image/microscopy, witness and community, what the entry adds to the archive)

The journal belongs first to the person writing it. The archive receives it only within the consent given. Nothing is public by default. Consent can narrow or expand later.

The template also sketches how Water Magicians can become a pattern for other small collectives: archive holder, second-language or systems lens, image/body/land/community witness, clear consent boundary, shared journal format.

## The Three-Strand Work

The shape forming from the collaboration:

1. **A private research archive** that preserves raw source, process variables, and observation language.
2. **A living collaboration record** that protects who saw what, who said what, and where shared understanding emerged.
3. **A future book or teaching container** that can draw from the first two only after review, consent, and careful attribution.

The first shared product is not the book, a membership, or a public archive. The first shared product is the living source field itself, kept clean enough that later books, gatherings, images, teachings, and public notes can grow from it.
