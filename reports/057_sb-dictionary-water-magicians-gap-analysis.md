## What The Dictionary Does Not Yet Hold

These are terms, distinctions, or conceptual moves that appear actively in the Water Magicians archive — in the Telegram analysis, the Fiona microscopy material, and the July 1 collaboration discussions — but have no home in the current Dictionary structure.

### 1. Microscopy observation vocabulary

The biggest gap. Fiona's work produces a distinct observational layer that the Dictionary has no entry for. The file `30-analysis/analysis-circulatio-microscopy.md` exists in the SB archive but the controlled vocabulary hasn't been extracted into the Dictionary.

Terms needing a `terms/microscopy.md` entry or equivalent:

| Term | Water Magicians context |
|---|---|
| Center / edge | distinguishing observation zones within the sample field |
| Motion / movement | live movement visible under magnification (distinct from the jar's static sediment record) |
| Vortex | dynamic rotational motion pattern in a sample |
| Branching | crystal or growth pattern radiating outward |
| Blank field | when the sample shows no distinguishing structure (an observation in itself) |
| Boundary | the visible edge between zones or phases within a sample (different from alchemical vessel boundary) |
| Media | what the sample is suspended in or placed on |
| Settling time | how long before observation; affects what is visible |
| Sample state | fresh, dried, aged — in the microscopy context, not the jar context |

These need register distinctions: the microscopy sense of "boundary" and "vortex" is literal rather than symbolic, and must be separated from the alchemical uses of both words that already exist in the Dictionary.

### 2. "Moving food fluid" as a material category

The June 27-28 Water Magicians thread — and explicitly named in the July 1 use map — produces a concept that sits beside plasma in the observational field: food, plant material, fermentation products, infusions, kitchen preparations, and living-water observations held in the same record alongside plasma observations.

The Plasma Forms Taxonomy covers plasma forms only. "Moving food fluid" is an emerging term for the larger class of living endogenous and plant-derived fluids that the practice involves. It has no home in the Dictionary. It may eventually become a short term page, or an entry in the Plasma Forms Taxonomy as a parallel category.

### 3. The uniqueness / non-categorization principle

The June 30 – July 1 Water Magicians exchange is the strongest conceptual contribution that could strengthen the Dictionary's `tradition-registers.md`. Megan's challenge that Shivambu cannot be systematically mapped onto Ayurvedic doshas, gunas, or elements, and Fiona's response from microscopy — "fluid, fractal, unique, moving food fluid," closer to prima materia than any fixed category — produces a principle that the current Tradition Registers entry on Ayurveda does not contain.

The current Ayurveda entry says Ayurvedic language "can clarify qualities: heavy/light, heating/cooling..." without saying what it cannot do. The Water Magicians exchange produced the complementary guardrail:

> Each instance must be held as specific before being placed in a category. Systematic categorization through doshas, gunas, or elements should follow sustained observation, not precede it.

This belongs either as an addition to the Ayurveda section of `tradition-registers.md`, or as a new brief entry — "Uniqueness / Non-categorization" — in the Dictionary.

### 4. Solar / Lunar as temporal registers

The Plasma Forms Taxonomy defines Solar and Lunar as fraction names (early volatile vs. middle clear). The Water Magicians archive uses these same words temporally: pre-dawn collection (solar timing), night timing, menstrual-lunar associations, seasonal rhythm. This dual use — fraction name and temporal/cyclical descriptor — is not disambiguated anywhere in the current Dictionary.

Without disambiguation, a reader cannot tell whether "Solar" means the early distillate fraction or a reference to time-of-collection or seasonal character. This needs a short note, either in the Plasma Forms Taxonomy under Spirit/Solar or in the Dictionary README quick legend.

### 5. Topping-up as a run technique

The Water Magicians archive identifies "topping-up versus separate runs" as a practical distillation variable. The Translation Map has cohobation (redistilling the pure output) but that is a different operation. Topping-up means replenishing the chamber with fresh or aged source material mid-run rather than running separate batches. It changes the character of the fractions and the retentate.

This could go into the Plasma Forms Taxonomy as a run-technique note, or into the method documents. It is currently unnamed anywhere in the Dictionary structure.

### 6. White ash / distillation chamber residue

The Water Magicians archive returns repeatedly to white ash as a visual observation — the dry chalky material left in the distillation chamber after a complete run. The Sediment Field Guide covers sediments in aging jars in detail but only briefly addresses post-distillation residue. It does not establish controlled vocabulary for what remains in the chamber.

This is adjacent to Mineral Salts and Serum/Saturn but not the same — it is what the Saturn fraction leaves behind after the distillation is fully complete and the chamber has dried. In the alchemical register this points toward VITRIOL and the Sal Salis operation, but neither of those entries in the Translation Map specifies the observable material.
