## What Needs Care

The packet has many direct practice instructions. For private working notes, this is fine. For archive prose in Ana's voice, the language needs to shift away from instructing the reader's body.

Examples of patterns to soften:

- "Begin on the floor or seated."
- "Ask gently..."
- "Notice..."
- "Use after a nightmare, conflict, panic..."
- "Do not demand..."

Safer archive framing:

- "A possible beginning is..."
- "The practice can begin with..."
- "The journal can ask..."
- "The body may be supported by..."
- "Meaning can wait until orientation returns."

This preserves usefulness without becoming directive.

The words `recovery`, `trauma`, `panic`, `nervous system`, `dorsal collapse`, `vagal tone`, and `co-regulation` make the packet health-adjacent and partly clinical in register. They are not forbidden, but they raise the sensitivity level. If the material moves toward community or public use, it needs clear non-clinical boundaries and probably a preference for the native language of the work: orientation, return, re-entry, support, relationship, and perception.

The translation maps are useful, but they should remain interpretive until sourced. Alchemy, Ayurveda, nervous system language, astrology, lunar rhythm, solar year, menstrual cycle, and dreamwork can be allowed to speak to one another, but the archive should avoid implying they are identical systems.

The `Shivambu Surfing Through The Heart` section should remain private for now. It touches Sovereign Biophysics and operational body-practice territory. It may be a bridge concept, but it should not be folded into a community Field Guide until Ana decides whether this work belongs in The Alchemy of Return, Sovereign Biophysics, or a private method lane.
