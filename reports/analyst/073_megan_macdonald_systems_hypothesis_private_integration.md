## Technical Feedback

Several anchors in the document are useful and defensible as background:

- Urine begins as a kidney-filtered plasma-derived ultrafiltrate, though final urine is heavily altered by tubular reabsorption and secretion.
- Clinical therapeutic plasma exchange really does remove or replace plasma components in medical settings, including pathogenic antibodies or other plasma-borne factors in specific indications.
- The pharmaceutical precedent is real: Premarin is derived from pregnant mare urine, and menotropins such as Repronex are extracted from postmenopausal urine.
- Urea has established dermatologic roles as a humectant, keratolytic, and barrier-supporting ingredient.
- Gut immune tissue, mucosal immunity, autonomic state, and endocrine regulation are real interconnected systems.

Several parts need containment before archive use:

- **"Endogenous plasma therapy"** is a useful phrase inside the community, but public use should clarify that this is not clinical therapeutic plasma exchange.
- **"Auto-inoculation" or "personalized vaccine"** should be treated as metaphor or hypothesis. It is too strong as mechanism language without immunological data.
- **"One Driver" inflammation** is too simple. It may be an organizing lens, but the Aether archive is showing source-state, cycle, diet, nervous system, household rhythm, aging, and fraction identity all acting together.
- **"Pregnenolone steal" and "cortisol steal"** should be avoided or softened. They are popular functional-medicine shorthand, not a clean endocrine mechanism.
- **"Proves non-placebo"** should be removed. The teen-skeptic case is interesting qualitative testimony. It is not proof.
- **"Disease reversal," "long-term efficacy," and "biological longevity"** should not enter Aether's public language without controlled evidence and medical review.
- **Hormone language needs fraction discipline.** Aether's current biochemistry note says steroid hormones do not meaningfully travel into the distillate and instead concentrate in the retentate/serum. Megan's broader endogenous-feedback language can fit raw or whole-material practice, but it cannot be mapped directly onto distilled spirit/essence without losing precision.
