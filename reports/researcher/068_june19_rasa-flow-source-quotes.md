# 68. June 19 Rasa Flow Source Quotes

## Executive Read

I did not find one clean traditional line that says the pure plasma returns to the body and directly wakes dormancy. The honest support is a braid:

- Armstrong gives the strongest historical urine-therapy language for returned urine as living substance.
- Ayurveda gives the cleanest language for rasa, soma, ojas, and rejuvenation as essence-fluid.
- Haṭha Yoga gives the direct dormant-force awakening language, but through prāṇa, bandha, and kuṇḍalinī rather than urine.
- Chinese medical qigong gives the best qì language for restored circulation, activation, and movement.

For the current SB essay, the most suitable supporting pair is one Ayurvedic rasa/rasāyana quote and one Armstrong quote, with the haṭha/qì material held as background unless the piece needs a more explicit "dormancy waking" bridge.

## Best Quote Pair

### 1. Rasa/Rasāyana Anchor

> Frawley & Lad, *The Yoga of Herbs*, Ch. 6, "Rejuvenative Tonics (Rasāyana karma)"
>
> "Rasayana is what enters ... in the essence (rasa)."

Use this where the essay names rasa as nourishment in motion. The surrounding passage says rasāyana penetrates and revitalizes the psycho-physiological essence. This supports the language of essence-fluid without making a direct claim about distillate.

Alternative from Frawley, *Soma in Yoga and Ayurveda*, Part IV:

> "rasa... means both 'fluid' and 'essence'."

This is the simplest bridge between plasma/rasa language and the essay's body of thought.

### 2. Returned Living Substance

> J. W. Armstrong, *The Water of Life*, Ch. 7
>
> "flesh, blood and vital tissues in living solution"

This is the strongest historical support for Ana's phrase "pure plasma" as a returned living source. It should be framed as Armstrong's urine-therapy claim, not as a verified medical statement.

Secondary Armstrong phrase, if a sentence needs movement:

> "cleanses... frees from obstruction... rebuilds"

This is useful only with boundary language. The surrounding passage is medically expansive and should not be imported whole.

## Dormancy And Waking

> *Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā*, Ch. 3
>
> "When the sleeping Kundalini awakens..."

The text also says the knots are pierced and the channels open. This is the direct "dormancy waking" source, but it belongs to haṭha physiology: mudrā, bandha, prāṇa, apāna, and suṣumṇā. It does not say urine or plasma causes the awakening.

Best use: a light background sentence, not the main proof. Example: "The older haṭha language speaks of dormant force awakening when prāṇa and apāna are brought into relation." Then let the essay return to Ana's observed field.

## Qì / Flow Support

> Jerry Alan Johnson, *Chinese Medical Qigong Therapy*, Vol. 2, Ch. 14
>
> "gather and disperse... rise and fall as it circulates"

The surrounding section describes qì as the body's vital life-force, with normal function as harmonious movement. This supports the "flow returns" language well, especially if the essay uses qì as a movement vocabulary rather than a claim of mechanism.

## Caraka Support

> *Carakasaṃhitā*, Sūtrasthāna 18.49 (P. V. Sharma, Vol. 1)
>
> "enthusiasm, inspiration and expiration, proper movement"

The surrounding verse names these as normal functions of vāta, along with maintaining dhātus in place and initiating activity. This is useful if the essay wants a classical Ayurvedic anchor for restored movement and animation.

For ojas:

> *Carakasaṃhitā*, Sūtrasthāna 17.73-75 (P. V. Sharma, Vol. 1)
>
> "ojas is produced first"

This supports ojas as a primary life-substance, but it is less immediately suited to the June 19 essay than the rasa/rasāyana material.

## Sources To Avoid In The Main Essay

The *Damar Tantra* has direct shivāmbu praise and nectar language, but it quickly turns absolute, curative, and immortality-heavy. It would add intensity without improving the archive's evidentiary integrity. I would hold it for a later historical appendix, not the main SB orientation piece.

## Recommended Placement

In `rasa-qi-restoration-flow.md`, the cleanest insertion would be:

- In the rasa section: use Frawley & Lad or Frawley on rasa as fluid/essence.
- Near the pure plasma reflection: use Armstrong as a historical witness to urine as living solution.
- If needed, add one sentence of background after the movement section: haṭha texts speak of dormant force waking through restored prāṇic relation; Chinese medical qigong speaks of qì as cyclical movement.

This keeps the piece alive while avoiding filler and avoiding stronger claims than the sources can carry.
