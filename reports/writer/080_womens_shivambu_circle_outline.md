# 080. Women's Shivambu Circle Outline

**Date:** 2026-07-09  
**Agent:** Codex  
**Session topic:** Draft outline and visual prompt set for the July 24, 2026 Women's Shivambu Circle

---

## Working Assumptions

- This is a live online community gathering on Friday, July 24, 2026 at 17:00 UTC.
- The hosts are Ana Seahawk, Fiona Gardner, and Megan Macdonald.
- The circle may be larger than an intimate call, so the structure needs to hold both presence and scale.
- The language should stay invitational, non-clinical, and grounded in direct experience.
- Image models should generate backgrounds without embedded text. Add text later in Canva, Keynote, PowerPoint, or similar so names, dates, and phrases stay correct.

## Container Tone

This first circle can be framed as a threshold rather than an announcement of a movement.

Suggested orientation:

> This is a gathering for women to speak honestly and carefully about Shivambu as lived experience: practice, cycles, body changes, questions, and discoveries. Nothing here replaces personal discernment or care. The circle is a place for witness, not prescription.

Shorter version:

> A quiet first circle for women exploring Shivambu through direct experience, personal discernment, and shared witness.

## Event Outline

### 1. Arrival Room: 5-10 Minutes

Hold the first minutes softly while people arrive.

Visual: the poster or a matching holding slide.

Music, if used, should be minimal and warm. Avoid anything that makes the room feel like a performance.

Overlay text:

> Women's Shivambu Circle  
> We will begin shortly.  
> Practice. Cycles. Body changes. Questions. Discoveries.

### 2. Welcome and Threshold: 5 Minutes

Ana opens by naming why this circle exists and why now.

Core points:

- This appears to be the first gathering of its kind in this immediate community field.
- The response so far suggests real readiness and curiosity.
- The room is not for persuasion.
- The room is for honest witness, careful language, and body autonomy.

Possible opening language:

> Welcome to this first Women's Shivambu Circle. We are here because something quiet has been gathering: women comparing notes, asking better questions, and wanting a space where direct experience can be spoken without being corrected, exaggerated, or turned into instruction.
>
> This circle is not here to persuade anyone. It is here to make room for personal record, shared discernment, and the dignity of each woman's own body.

### 3. Grounding the Room: 5 Minutes

Megan leads a short grounding meditation before the circle agreements. This gives the room a shared breath before the subject is named in more detail.

Keep the meditation simple, optional, and consent-based.

Possible frame:

> Megan will take us through a short grounding now. Let this be simple. Eyes open or closed, seated or standing, present in whatever way is available.

Suggested arc:

- Arrive with the breath.
- Feel the support underneath the body.
- Let attention come into the room.
- Make space for curiosity without pressure.
- Return to the sovereignty of each body.

Overlay text:

> Arrive.  
> Soften.  
> Listen.

### 4. Safety and Circle Agreements: 7 Minutes

Keep this simple and explicit.

Suggested agreements:

- Speak from personal experience.
- No medical advice or treatment claims.
- No pressure to share.
- No recording or screenshots unless explicitly agreed.
- Hold names and stories with care.
- Curiosity is welcome. Correction and debate can wait.

Overlay text:

> Speak from "I."  
> Let the body remain sovereign.  
> Let the circle remain kind.

### 5. Three-Host Opening Arc: 13 Minutes

The three hosts enter by function, not biography. Ana names the threshold, Megan carries the embodied thread, and Fiona names the wider field.

Ana: why now, why this circle.

Suggested focus:

- The response already gathering around this subject.
- The need for a careful women-only room.
- The boundary that this is witness, not prescription.

Megan: what it means to enter the subject through the body.

Suggested focus:

- Why grounding matters before speaking about a charged or tender subject.
- How a settled room changes what can be received.
- The invitation to stay simple, present, and self-led.

Fiona: the field we are naming.

Suggested focus:

- Women carrying body knowledge privately and relationally.
- The tenderness, taboo, curiosity, and courage around Shivambu.
- The value of a room where questions can be held without being rushed into answers.

Shared prompt for all three hosts:

- What brought me here.
- What I am holding for this circle.
- One question I am still living with.

Slide text:

> Three Women Holding the Circle  
> Ana Seahawk  
> Fiona Gardner  
> Megan Macdonald  
> What brought me here. What I am holding. What I am still asking.

### 6. The Field We Are Naming: Built Into the Opening Arc

This portion can be led primarily by Fiona, with Ana and Megan adding one brief thread each. It does not need to feel like a separate talk. It can be the woven opening that moves the room from arrival into shared inquiry.

Possible themes:

- Women have often carried body knowledge in private.
- Some practices survive through whispers, households, lineages, and lived necessity.
- Shivambu can bring up tenderness, taboo, curiosity, resistance, and clarity.
- A women-only circle gives the subject a different nervous system.

Possible language:

> Some forms of body knowledge do not enter the room through authority. They arrive through stories, hesitation, private experiments, old texts, grandmothers, dreams, kitchens, illness, recovery, and the quiet courage to ask a question out loud.
>
> Tonight we are not trying to settle the subject. We are giving it a clean room.

### 7. Practice, Cycles, Body Changes, Questions, Discoveries: 25 Minutes

This can be the heart of the gathering. Use the five poster words as the structure.

Ana or the hosts can move through each word briefly, then invite reflection.

Practice:

- How women have approached the practice.
- What kinds of boundaries or rhythms matter.
- What made the practice feel possible or not possible.

Cycles:

- Menstrual, lunar, seasonal, emotional, creative, and life-stage rhythms.
- Keep it observational, not prescriptive.

Body changes:

- Use careful language: "changes women have noticed," not "effects."
- Encourage specificity without generalizing.

Questions:

- What remains unclear.
- What people wish had been named earlier.
- What needs research, lineage, or more careful conversation.

Discoveries:

- Moments of surprise.
- New respect for the body.
- New boundaries.
- New language.

Slide text:

> Practice  
> Cycles  
> Body Changes  
> Questions  
> Discoveries

Facilitator bridge:

> We are going to move through these as doorways, not conclusions.

### 8. Participant Reflection: 15 Minutes

If turnout is large, use chat prompts first. Then invite a few voices live.

Chat prompts:

- One word for what brought you here.
- One question you are carrying.
- One place where you want more careful language.
- One thing you want this circle to protect.

Live-share prompt:

> Speak from your own body and your own story. A few sentences are enough.

### 9. Questions for the Hosts: 7 Minutes

The hosts can answer from lived experience and boundaries, not authority.

Useful response forms:

- "In my own experience..."
- "What I can speak to is..."
- "I would keep that as a personal discernment question."
- "That would need a different kind of source or practitioner support than this circle can provide."

Slide text:

> Questions  
> Personal experience. Careful language. No prescriptions.

### 10. Closing Round: 3 Minutes

Invite everyone to leave with one phrase, either in chat or silently.

Prompt:

> What are you taking with you from this circle?

Host close:

> Thank you for helping make a room where this subject can be spoken carefully. May what is useful remain simple. May what is not yours pass by. May each body keep its own authority.

Final slide text:

> The body remains sovereign.  
> The circle continues through care.

## 90-Minute Run of Show

- Arrival: 10
- Welcome and threshold: 5
- Megan grounding meditation: 5
- Circle agreements: 7
- Three-host opening arc: 13
- Five doorways: 25
- Participant reflection: 15
- Questions: 7
- Closing: 3

Total: 90 minutes.

This gives Megan a natural grounding role near the beginning, gives Fiona a substantive place in the field-naming portion, and keeps Ana's opening focused on threshold, purpose, and boundaries. If the room is full, the participant reflection can lean on chat first, then a small number of brief live voices.
