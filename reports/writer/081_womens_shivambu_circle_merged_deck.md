# 081. Women's Shivambu Circle Merged Deck

**Date:** 2026-07-16  
**Agent:** Codex  
**Session topic:** Merged slide draft from `080_womens_shivambu_circle_outline.md` and the Downloads Women's Circle layout

---

## Purpose

This draft keeps the contemplative-card feeling of the Downloads deck and the
container discipline of report 080.

The screen should feel like the circle is holding the room. Speaker names can
stay in the run-of-show and speaker notes, but they do not need to appear on
the slides.

## Design Rules

- No visible `Speaker:` labels.
- No repeated host-name footer.
- The first slide may name the three hosts once, or the hosts can be named
  aloud only.
- Use the Downloads visual style: split card, luminous water, cream, pearl,
  pale gold, watery blue, moon phases, botanical detail.
- Keep slide text spacious. The slides hold atmosphere; the hosts hold the
  spoken detail.
- Keep the safety language explicit. This circle needs a clean container.
- Avoid medical language, treatment claims, and instruction to the body.

## Suggested 90-Minute Flow

| Segment | Time | Lead |
|---|---:|---|
| Arrival / room opens | 5-10 min | holding slide |
| Welcome and threshold | 5 min | Ana |
| Wonder / field permission | 7 min | Fiona |
| Grounding and agreements | 8 min | Megan |
| Shivambu as doorway | 8 min | Ana |
| Five doorways | 20 min | Ana / all hosts |
| Circle opens | 17 min | Ana hosts, Megan timekeeping, Fiona field witness |
| Questions and reflections | 10 min | hosts |
| Wonder remains | 5 min | Fiona |
| Closing | 5 min | Ana |

Total: 90 minutes, with the arrival room included as the first 5-10 minutes.

## Slide 2: Threshold

**Use:** Ana opens the room.

**Visible text:**

> A first circle.  
> A careful room.  
> Witness, not prescription.

**Speaker cue: Ana**

Welcome to this first Women's Shivambu Circle.

Something quiet has been gathering: women comparing notes, asking better
questions, and wanting a room where direct experience can be spoken without
being corrected, exaggerated, or turned into instruction.

This circle is not here to persuade anyone. It is here for personal record,
shared discernment, and the dignity of each woman's own body.

**Image direction:** a soft threshold made of water and light. No central
figure. The feeling is arrival, not intensity.

## Slide 3: Wonder

**Use:** Fiona opens curiosity and permission before the practical container.

**Visible text:**

> Wonder  
>
> Before practice.  
> Before belief.  
> Before conclusion.  
>
> A woman looks.  
> The body answers in its own time.  
> Curiosity is enough.

**Speaker cue: Fiona**

I want to begin with wonder.

Before deciding what something means, there is a moment when a woman simply
looks. She notices what is present. She lets curiosity arrive before judgment.

For many women, this is not a small thing. The body has often been met through
shame, distance, discomfort, or fear.

This room gives curiosity a place to begin again.

**Image direction:** a woman seated in a luminous watery space, holding or
looking into a bowl of clear water. Tender, ordinary, unguarded, not glamorous.

**Reference image:** `2-Contemplative serenity and cosmic wonder.png`, but
remove the visible speaker name and footer.

## Slide 4: Grounding

**Use:** Megan settles the room before agreements.

**Visible text:**

> Arrive.  
> Soften.  
> Listen.

**Speaker cue: Megan**

We will take a short moment to arrive together.

Eyes may be open or closed. Sitting, standing, moving slightly, or staying still
are all welcome. The purpose is simple: to let the room become steady enough for
honest speech.

Let attention come into the space. Let there be curiosity without pressure.
Each body remains its own.

**Image direction:** still water, moon reflection, gentle ripples, no people,
open text field.

## Slide 5: Circle Agreements

**Use:** Megan names the practical container.

**Visible text:**

> Circle Agreements  
>
> Speak from personal experience.  
> No medical advice or treatment claims.  
> No pressure to share.  
> No recording or screenshots unless explicitly agreed.  
> Hold names and stories with care.  
> Curiosity is welcome. Correction and debate can wait.

**Speaker cue: Megan**

This circle is held by a few simple agreements.

We speak from personal experience. We do not give medical advice or make
treatment claims. No one is required to share, agree, or know.

Names and stories stay held with care. Recording and screenshots happen only if
the room has explicitly agreed.

Curiosity belongs here. Correction and debate can wait.

**Image direction:** women gathered in a simple, non-hierarchical circle, or an
abstract circle of stones and water. The text needs enough quiet space to remain
readable.

**Reference image:** `3-Serene safety gathering under moonlight.png`, but the
slide text should be the full agreements from report 080, and the speaker name
and footer should be removed.

## Slide 6: Shivambu as a Doorway

**Use:** Ana names the subject without turning it into a requirement.

**Visible text:**

> Shivambu as a Doorway  
>
> Not a conclusion.  
> Not a requirement.  
> Not a belief to adopt.  
>
> A doorway into observation.  
> A doorway into curiosity.  
> A doorway into direct experience.

**Speaker cue: Ana**

In this circle, Shivambu is held as a doorway.

The doorway may bring up curiosity, resistance, memory, discomfort,
recognition, fear, interest, or tenderness. All of that belongs to the field of
observation.

Before any practice, before any belief, there is already something to notice:
what the body has been taught to reject, what it has been taught to hide, and
what it may be ready to meet differently.

The point is not for every woman to walk through the doorway in the same way.
The point is to notice what the doorway reveals.

**Image direction:** water-and-light threshold, moon, ripples, luminous opening.
A small quiet figure may be present, but the threshold should be primary.

**Reference image:** `4-A portal toward observation.png`, with visible names
removed.

## Slide 7: Five Doorways

**Use:** main conversation structure.

**Visible text:**

> Practice  
> Cycles  
> Body Changes  
> Questions  
> Discoveries

Small line:

> Doorways, not conclusions.

**Speaker cue: Ana / hosts**

We will move through five doorways: practice, cycles, body changes, questions,
and discoveries.

These are not conclusions. They are ways to let the conversation find shape.

Practice can include what has felt possible or not possible. Cycles can include
menstrual, lunar, seasonal, emotional, creative, or life-stage rhythms. Body
changes means changes women have noticed, held carefully and without
generalizing. Questions can remain open. Discoveries can be small.

**Image direction:** five soft openings, pools, or moonlit doorways arranged as
a quiet sequence. No figures needed.

## Slide 8: The Circle Is Open

**Use:** participant reflection begins.

**Visible text:**

> The Circle Is Open  
>
> Speak from the "I."  
> Write in the chat.  
> Raise your hand to speak.  
> Passing is welcome.  
> Listening is participation.

**Speaker cue: Ana**

The circle is open now.

Because we are online, the chat is part of the circle. Everyone has a place
here, whether speaking aloud, writing, or staying in quiet witness.

One thread is enough. Passing is welcome.

Prompts, if useful:

- What brought you here?
- What are you curious about?
- What question are you carrying?
- What would you like this circle to hold with care?

**Image direction:** a circle of women or luminous water opening at the center.
The image should feel spacious, not ceremonial or staged.

**Reference image:** `5-Mystical opening of a sacred circle.png`, with the
visible speaker name and footer removed.

## Slide 9: What Does This Bring Up?

**Use:** first deeper reflection after the room has spoken once.

**Visible text:**

> What does this bring up?  
>
> Curiosity  
> Resistance  
> Memory  
> Wonder  
> Fear  
> Recognition  
>
> There is no right answer.  
> Only what is here.

**Speaker cue: Ana**

Now that the room has begun to speak, we can ask a very simple question.

What does this bring up?

The answer does not need to be tidy. It may be curiosity, resistance, memory,
wonder, fear, recognition, discomfort, tenderness, or uncertainty.

This question does not ask anyone to explain herself. It gives what is present
a place.

**Image direction:** still water under moonlight with several gentle ripples,
as if many responses are entering the same field.

**Reference image:** `6-Celestial reflections and botanical whispers.png`, with
visible speaker name and footer removed.

## Slide 10: Questions and Reflections

**Use:** shared questions before closing.

**Visible text:**

> Questions & Reflections  
>
> What wants to be asked?  
> What wants to be named?  
> What wants to be witnessed?  
>
> No need to solve.  
> No need to conclude.

**Speaker cue: hosts**

This does not need to become a formal Q&A.

A question, a reflection, a feeling, or something unfinished can all belong
here. The hosts can answer from lived experience and boundaries, not authority.

Useful response forms:

- In my own experience...
- What I can speak to is...
- I would keep that as a personal discernment question.
- That asks for a different kind of support than this circle can provide.

**Image direction:** several streams meeting in one pool, moonlit, gold
ripples, no central figure.

**Reference image:** `7-Questions and reflections in moonlight.png`, with the
visible speaker name and footer removed.

## Slide 11: Wonder Remains

**Use:** Fiona begins the closing sequence by witnessing the field.

**Visible text:**

> Wonder Remains  
>
> Answers can wait.  
>
> Something changes  
> when women are allowed  
> to look without shame.  
>
> Curiosity is not small.

**Speaker cue: Fiona**

I am noticing how much can open when women have room to speak honestly about
the body.

Curiosity itself is already a movement toward relationship. The room does not
need to rush toward answers.

Sometimes the first opening is being able to look, listen, and wonder without
shame.

That matters.

**Image direction:** hands holding a bowl of water, no face emphasis, warm
light, quiet, humble, reflective.

**Reference image:** `8-Wonder remains: a peaceful offering.png`.

## Slide 12: Closing the Circle

**Use:** final round and close.

**Visible text:**

> What are you leaving with?  
>
> One word.  
> One feeling.  
> One image.  
> One thread to carry.  
>
> The body remains sovereign.  
> The circle continues through care.

**Speaker cue: Ana**

To close, we will hear one word, one feeling, one image, or one thread you are
leaving with.

There is no need to explain unless that feels right. Silence is also welcome.

Thank you for what was spoken, what was written, and what was held quietly.
May each woman leave with what is hers, and leave behind what is not hers to
carry.

The body remains sovereign. The circle continues through care.

**Image direction:** a single luminous drop returning to a calm pool, soft
moonlight, completion without finality.

**Reference image:** `9-A serene night of reflection.png`, but remove the host
names at the top if the image is remade.
