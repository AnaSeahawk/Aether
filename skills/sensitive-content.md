# Skill — sensitive content

*Closed-by-default handling for private, operational, and publishing-sensitive material.*

---

## What this skill is for

Use this skill before inspecting, editing, summarizing, moving, publishing, or
cross-referencing sensitive material in Aether.

Sensitive material includes:

- `Components/website/sovereign-biophysics-distillation/`
- private method, protocol, experiment, or body-record material
- health-adjacent claims, practices, or observations
- collaboration-private material, including private Water Magicians files
- unpublished drafts not explicitly approved for public or community use
- personal, relational, financial, identity, credential, or access details

---

## Core rule

Private is the default.

Do not publish, quote, summarize into a public-facing file, promote visibility,
or move sensitive content into a broader-access location without explicit user
approval for that exact action.

Metadata such as `visibility: public` or `visibility: community` means only that
the file is a candidate for review. It is not publication approval.

---

## Before touching sensitive content

1. Confirm the task actually requires opening the sensitive file.
2. Claim the narrowest path needed through `tools/orchestrate`.
3. Keep any notes or reports in the appropriate private or role-owned report
   lane.
4. Preserve single-subject framing: personal record and responsibility only.
5. Avoid medical directives, treatment claims, or instructions to the reader's
   body.

If the task can be done from filenames, metadata, or an existing report, do not
open the more sensitive body text.

---

## Publishing boundary

Nothing sensitive is published without explicit approval from Ana.

Before any sensitive file moves toward publication, confirm:

- the file is meant to leave the private archive;
- its frontmatter supports review (`visibility: community` or `public`);
- the review queue and publish checklist are updated;
- the language makes personal observation clear and avoids directives;
- no private collaborator, access, credential, or operational details are exposed.

When in doubt, stop and ask. Do not infer publication intent from proximity,
folder name, prior reports, or another agent's suggestion.

---

## Report hygiene

Reports may name a sensitive file path when needed for continuity. Keep the
substance minimal:

- describe the action needed;
- avoid copying sensitive passages unless the report itself is private and the
  quote is necessary;
- record blockers and approvals clearly;
- do not make stale reports look current.

If existing report guidance about sensitivity is outdated, follow `AGENTS.md`
stale-report handling: replace it with a new numbered report instead of editing
the stale report in place.
